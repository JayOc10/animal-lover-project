Tips for Adoption:

Be in the Know!

Bringing home a new pet is an exciting time, but it can also cause an upheaval in your household. Before you welcome home that adorable pet you found in an animal shelter or rescue, take the time to prepare your home, your family, and your current pet for your new addition.

Happy Home, Happy Pet!

Once youâ€™ve decided on the pet who will best fit your household, create separate areas for your new and current pets. This will allow them to have their own spaces to relax without fighting over resources. In the beginning, you may need to place baby gates to keep the areas truly separate to introduce the pets slowly. For example, cats often need more time than dogs to adapt to change, so outfit a spare bedroom or office for your new cat. Place a litter box, food and water dishes, bedding, and climbing tower for maximum comfort, and allow your cats to sniff each other under the door until they appear comfortable with each other.

All about the Love!

Our furry family members need love too, so shower your pet in hugs and kisses! Our pets are always there for us in our times of need so it's only fair that we return the favor to them. So grab your pet's favorite treats, toys, etc. and bond with them. Have fun!

Every year, approximately 8 to 12 millions animals enter animal shelters nationwide, and approximately 5 to 9 million pets are euthanized. Currently, the average length of stay in a shelter is eight days, however there are many cases in which animals stay as long as eight to nine months before adoption. Animals are often given uo or found on the streets and are unable to get the care or family they need because shelters are too full. Our shelters need your help so that every animal can get the proper care and attention they need.
